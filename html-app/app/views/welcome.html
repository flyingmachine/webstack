Gratiteful Place

Happiness is not something that you pursue; it is something you allow.

— Chade Meng-Tan

Most of us were taught that happiness can only be won by effort. Pursue your dreams, rack up accomplishments, get everything just right and – the thinking goes – you will be happy.

Except that's not how it works. You've probably heard about winning the lottery does not make people permaanently happier. In general, research shows that we adjust to positive changes to our circumstances, leaving us about as happy as before.

Getting a raise, buying a house — these events only have a short-term impact on happiness. There's even a spiffy name for this phenomenon: the "hedonic treadmill."

Stepping Off the Treadmill

Research shows that one way to achieve a lasting increaase in happiness is to cultivate a sense of gratitude. This can be as simple as taking a few minutes every day to write down a few things you're grateful for.

Think of it this way: why should you put conditions on your happiness? Instead of instead of believing that life be a certain away before you feel OK, you can learn to be happy with life exactly as it is.
